In Part Three you will be offered six different choice problems. If you are selected for payment in Part Three, we will randomly select one of your six decisions for payment; whichever choice you made in that randomly selected problem will be implemented for payment.

For each of the six different choice problems, we will offer you a choice across four lotteries, with a tradeoff between risk and reward.

  • Lottery A will always be the safest lottery, where the lowest possible prize from the lottery always exceeds $8.
  • Lotteries B, C and D have an increasing chance of the lowest prize ($8).
  • However, Lotteries B, C and D will also have increasing chances of higher prize amounts.
  • Lottery D will always be the riskiest lottery of the four, with the largest probability on the lowest prize ($8).

To help you make your choice here, as you click on each lottery, we will illustrate the chances of each prize amount in a graph. Once you are happy with you choice for each round you should hit "Next".
