{{ block title }} {{ endblock }} {{ block content }} Player A will make an offer first. Player B will receive this offer. Each Player will have a point that gives them the highest possible payoff. For Player A, this value is always 25. Player B will have an optimal point between one and twenty. This is the result of the roll of three twenty-sided dice. Their value will be determined by one of three possibilities:
  1. The minimum of the three dice rolls
  2. The median of the three dice rolls
  3. The maximum of the three dice rolls
Each of three possibilities gives a different probability that each optimal point is achieved. Below you can choose which of the three types that Player B will draw from, and then move the slider to see the probability that they will have that optimal point.
During the real experiment after the practice rounds, the distribution will be determined for you, but you can choose it here for illustrative purposes.
