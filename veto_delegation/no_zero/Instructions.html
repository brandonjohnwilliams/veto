Part Two is identical to Part One, except that the Buyer no longer has the option to walk away (purchase zero widgets).

Your First Role: Seller

You will first act as the Seller. You will make three choices, one for each urn type (Low, Middle, and High).

  • {{ if single == 1 }} You will choose a Single offer (a specific number of widgets) to propose. {{ else }} You will choose a Menu offer (a minimum and maximum number of widgets) to propose. {{ endif }}
  • The Buyer you are matched with must purchase {{ if single == 1 }} the number of widgets offered; {{ else }} an amount of widgets from the offered menu; {{ endif }} they cannot choose to walk away in Part Two (zero widgets).
  • Each of your three Seller choices will be matched to a different Buyer participant in the room.

Your Second Role: Buyer

After making your decisions as the Seller, you will then act as the Buyer for three different Seller participants, one for each urn type.

  • Because you cannot walk away, you must {{ if single == 1 }} purchase the number of widgets provided offered by the Seller.
  • You will see your drawn ideal quantity (X) from the given urn before viewing your outcome.
    • {{ else }} purchase an amount of widgets on the Seller's offered menu.
  • You will see your drawn ideal quantity (X) from the given urn before making your choice.
    • {{ endif }}

Payment for Part Two

The computer will randomly select one of the three urns and one participant in the room as the Seller for payment. The participant matched as the Buyer for that Seller/urn will also be selected for payment.

  • Seller Payment: Depends only on the number of widgets {{ if single == 1 }} offered. {{ else }} chosen from the offered menu. {{ endif }}
  • Buyer Payment: Depends on the number of widgets purchased and the ideal quantity (X) drawn from the selected urn.
