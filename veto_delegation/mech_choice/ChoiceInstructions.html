In Part Five, we are interested in which of the two experiments you would prefer to be paid for, fixing your Seller decisions. For each urn type, your decisions in Parts Three and Four lead to:

  • A payoff of $A from your decision to propose a single offer {{ if single == 1 }}(in Part Three){{ else }}(in Part Four){{ endif }}.
  • A payoff of $B from your decision to propose a menu of offers {{ if single == 1 }}(in Part Four){{ else }}(in Part Three){{ endif }}.

Which of these amounts do you think is larger? And by how much?

The Choice List

To address these questions, in the table below we ask you to decide which of these two amounts ($A or $B) you would prefer. By adding extra bonus money to one side or the other, we want to understand by how much you prefer one option over the other as the Seller.

  • Option A (Left Side) will always give you the average expected payment from your single offer {{ if single == 1 }} (your choice from Part Three){{ else }} (your choice from Part Four){{ endif }}. In the first five rows, we add extra bonus money this amount.
  • Option B (Right Side) will always give you the average expected payment from your menu of offers {{ if single == 0 }} (your choice from Part Four){{ else }} (your choice from Part Three){{ endif }}. In the last five rows, we add extra bonus money to this amount.

How to Make Your Choice

We want you to make a choice for each row in the table. For example, in the first row, you decide between your single offer outcome plus $5 ($A + $5) or just your menu outcome ($B). As we move down the table, Option A loses its bonus, hits zero, and then Option B begins gaining a bonus. By the last row, you are deciding between just your single offer outcome ($A) or your menu outcome plus $5 ($B + $5).

Because the right-hand column gets better as you move down the table, there is presumably a row where your preference switches from Option A to Option B. To save time, you only need to select the single row where you would switch. The interface will automatically fill out the rest of the table choices based on your switching point.

  • If you think the menu of offers outcome ($B) is better than the single offer outcome + $5, you should select the very first row (meaning you always take Option B).
  • If you think the single offer outcome ($A) is better than the menu of offers outcome + $5, you should select the very last row (meaning you always take Option A).

Text at the bottom of the page will automatically update to explain exactly what your choice means in words. Please test out the interface and make an example choice below.
