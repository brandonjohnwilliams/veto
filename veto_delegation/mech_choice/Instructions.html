In Part Four, you will see three different choice problems. If you are selected for payment in Part Four, we will randomly select one of your three decisions for payment; whichever choice you made in that randomly selected problem will be implemented for payment.

In previous sessions of this experiment, rather than a single offer in Part One, Sellers were allowed to offer a menu of options for the Buyer, a minimum and maximum number of widgets, that they made available for purchase to the Buyer. Buyers could choose to purchase a number of Widgets from the offered menu, or purchase zero Widgets. The Seller's menu could have a minimum and maximum down to 1 and up to 8, or any value in between. The menu available to the Buyer was any number between the minimum and maximum selection of the menu, and the option to purchase zero Widgets. Notice that just as in your session, the Seller could never remove the option for the Buyer to purchase zero Widgets.

On the next page, you will be able to explore the interface that the Sellers used in those sessions. You will also receive additional instructions about how the Seller could make their choice.
