Robot Buyers

In Part One, there was a degree of randomness in the outcome driven by:

  • Different Buyer ideal quantities (X) drawn from the urn.
  • Different participants in the room you were matched with.

The Robot Buyers in Part Three have been trained using Buyer decisions from {{ if single == 1 }}66{{ else }}64{{ endif }} participants who completed Part One in prior sessions. Because of this training, you should think about each Robot Buyer as acting like the Buyer participants you matched with in Part One.

For any {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} you propose, averaging across the Robot Buyers' response is effectively averaging over:

  • Different Buyer ideal quantities (X) drawn from the given urn.
  • Different decisions made by the {{ if single == 1 }}66{{ else }}64{{ endif }} prior participants given an ideal quantity
    • {{ if single == 1 }}Either taking your offer, {{ else }}Either choosing a particular option from your menu, {{ endif}} or walking away.

Your Task

Using your experience from Part One to guide you, your task is to choose {{ if single == 1 }}the offer{{ else }}the menu{{ endif }} that you believe will generate the highest average Seller payment. Note that:

  • The different urns affect how likely each ideal quantity is for each Robot Buyer, and therefore affect the average payment from any decision.
  • While Robot Buyer's decisions will be affected by the ideal quantities and Buyer payoffs (because of their training), no other participants are affected by your Seller decisions.

Part Three Payment

Three different participants in the room will be selected for payment in Part Three, one for each of the three types of urn.

  • Payment Amount: If selected, your payment will be the average Seller payment, calculated by providing your {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} to many Robot Buyers and averaging.
  • Because of this, it is in your interest to {{ if single == 1 }}make the offer{{ else }} select the menu{{ endif }} that you think will maximize the average Seller payment.
