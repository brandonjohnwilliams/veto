In Part Three we restore the option to walk away for the Buyer. However, we will now replace Buyer participants you interacted with in Part One with Robot Buyers trained to act like those participants. By matching you to a very large number of these robots and taking averages, for any decision you will make we can remove any randomness and offer you a fixed payment. Your role in Part Three is to make the decisions that you think will maximize your payment.

In Part Three of the experiment you will only make decisions as the Seller, choosing {{ if single == 1 }} an amount of widgets {{ else }} a menu of widgets {{ endif }} to offer to the Buyer just as in Part One. In Part One for any decision you made there was a degree of randomness in your round payoff as the Seller. This randomness was driven by:

  • the ideal quantity X drawn by the Buyer from the urn
  • the particular Buyer decisions made by the participant in the room you were matched to
In Part Three we remove this randomness. If selected for Payment in a Part Three decision, each specific decision you make will yield a fixed additional payment amount. This fixed payment amount is the average expected payment from your decision over the {{ if single == 1 }} offer you make for the given urn. {{ else }} menu you offer for the given urn. {{ endif }}

Therefore, by taking averages across a large number of Robot Buyers we can remove any randomness in the Seller's payment for any decision.
