In Part Two of the experiment you will again make decisions as a Seller, choosing {{ if single == 1 }} an amount of widgets {{ else }} a menu of widgets {{ endif }} to offer to the Buyer. However, whereas in Part One there was some randomness in your payoff as the Seller—based on the particular Buyer participant you were matched with that round, and the particular ideal quantity they drew from the urn—in Part Two we will instead pay you the average expected payment from your decision.

To achieve this, instead of being matched to another participant in the room, you will be matched to a large number of Robot Buyers, and paid the average Seller payment across those matches. Each Robot Buyer has been trained to reflect our best guess for behavior across [56] different Buyer participants in earlier sessions, where those participants made choices in an identical Part One. Because of this training, for any {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} you select and any ideal quantity drawn from the urn, the distribution of decisions across the different Robot Buyers reflects our best guess of the distribution of decisions across the [56] prior participants in that situation.

For any {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} you select in the Part Two decisions, you can think of your payment as the average outcome across millions of different Robot Buyers. Each Robot Buyer represents a fair draw of an ideal quantity from the given urn and a fair draw from the behavior of one of the [56] participants in the prior sessions.

Because the payment rule averages across such a large number of Robot Buyers, there is no longer any randomness in payment for any Seller choice. Any possible {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} you might choose leads to a fixed payment, where that payment reflects our best guess on the average outcome for the Seller payment across the [56] prior participants and the 20 different balls that could be drawn from the urn to determine the Buyer’s ideal quantity.

Given this rule, your role in Part Two is to choose the {{ if single == 1 }} offer {{ else }} menu {{ endif }} that you think leads to the highest expected Seller payment. You will make a separate decision over a {{ if single == 1 }} offer to make {{ else }} menu to offer {{ endif }} for each of the three urns: Low, Middle, and High.
