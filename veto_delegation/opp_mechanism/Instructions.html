In Part Three, your payment will again be calculated as the average outcome across a very large number of Robot Buyers, just like in Part Two.

However, there is one key difference: your choices in Part Three are based on a different version of this experiment.

{{ if single == 1 }} Where you previously chose a minimum and maximum quantity to offer (a menu), in this alternate version you will offer a single, fixed number of widgets to the Buyer.

As before, the Buyer can either choose the offered amount or walk away (zero widgets). Your choice as the Seller is simply which quantity to offer, from 1 to 8. {{ else }} Where you previously chose a single, fixed amount of widgets to offer, in this alternate version you will offer a minimum and maximum number of widgets to the Buyer (a menu).

As before, the Buyer can choose any amount of widgets within your offered menu, or walk away (zero widgets). Your choice as the Seller is to set both the minimum and maximum quantity of the offer, from 1 to 8. {{ endif }}

Notice that just as in your session, you can never remove the option for the Buyer to walk away (zero widgets).
