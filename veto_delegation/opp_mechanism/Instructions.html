In Part Four, your payment will again be calculated using the average Seller payment across a large number of Robot Buyers, just like in Part Three. However, your decisions in Part Four will be based on an alternate version of this experiment.

Rules in the Alternate Experiment

{{ if single == 1 }} Where you previously chose a minimum and maximum quantity to offer (a menu), in this alternate version you will offer a single, fixed number of widgets to the Buyer. {{ else }} Where you previously chose a single, fixed amount of widgets to offer, in this alternate version you will offer a minimum and maximum number of widgets to the Buyer, a menu of choices. {{ endif }}

  • The Buyer draws an ideal quantity (X) from the given urn exactly as before.
  • Just like in Part One, the Buyer can choose to walk away (purchase zero widgets) regardless of the Seller's decision.
  • {{ if single == 1 }} However, rather than choosing any amount from the offered menu, the Buyer's only option is to either buy the single fixed number of widgets or to walk away. {{ else }} However, rather than accepting a single fixed offer, the Buyer potentially has additional options over walking away, allowing them to choose any amount on the offered menu (between the selected minimum and maximum). {{ endif }}

On the next pages, you will be able to experiment with the interface used by the Sellers and Buyers in this alternate version of the experiment.
