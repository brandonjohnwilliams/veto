In Part Four, your payment will again be calculated using the average expected outcome as the Seller across a large number of Robot Buyers, just like in Part Three. However, your decisions in Part Four will be based on an alternate version of this experiment.

Rules in Alternate Experiment

{{ if single == 1 }} Where you previously chose a minimum and maximum quantity to offer (a menu), in this alternate version you will offer a single, fixed number of widgets to the Buyer. {{ else }} Where you previously chose a single, fixed amount of widgets to offer, in this alternate version you will offer a minimum and maximum number of widgets to the Buyer (a menu). {{ endif }}

  • The Buyer draws an ideal quantity (X) from the given urn exactly as before.
  • Just like in Part One, the Buyer could choose to walk away (purchase zero widgets) regardless of the Seller's decision.
  • {{ if single == 1 }} However, rather than choosing any amount from the offered menu, the Buyer's only option beyond walking away was to choose the Seller's fixed offer. {{ else }} However, rather than accepting a fixed offer, the Buyer potentially has additional options over walking, allowing them to choose any amount between the Seller's offered minimum and maximum. {{ endif }}

On the next page, you will be able to experiment with the interface used by the Sellers and Buyers in this alternate version of the experiment.
